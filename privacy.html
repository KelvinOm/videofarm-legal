Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 22, 2026

This Privacy Policy applies to VideoFarm (the "app"), a personal, self-hosted video-generation and publishing tool run by a single operator on their own hardware. The app does not operate a hosted server, does not onboard external users, and does not share data with any third party beyond the social media platforms the operator has explicitly connected.

1. Data stored locally by the app

To function, the app stores the following data locally on the operator's own machine:

  • OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens for connected accounts (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok).
  • Generated video files, scripts, thumbnails, and metadata.
  • Application logs and pipeline state.

None of this data is transmitted to the developer or to any third party other than the social media platforms the operator has explicitly connected, and only for the purpose of publishing content the operator chose to publish.

2. TikTok data specifically

When the operator connects a TikTok account, the app accesses the following data via TikTok's official APIs:

  • Basic profile info (via scope user.info.basic): TikTok open_id, display name, and avatar URL.
  • OAuth tokens: a TikTok access token and refresh token, stored exclusively on the operator's local machine in a file under state/tiktok_token.json. These tokens never leave the operator's machine except to be sent back to TikTok's own API endpoints.
  • Video upload capability (via scopes video.upload and video.publish): the app uses these scopes to upload videos that the operator has generated to their own TikTok account. No TikTok content is read, scraped, or retained beyond what the operator themselves published.

The app does not read the operator's TikTok follower list, private messages, direct messages, comments, or any other TikTok user data.

3. How to revoke the app's access

The operator can revoke the app's access to their TikTok account at any time:

  • On the TikTok mobile app: Profile → ☰ (menu) → Settings and privacy → Security & Permissions → Manage app permissions, then remove VideoFarm.
  • Equivalent flows exist for YouTube (Google account permissions) and Instagram (Manage access).

Once revoked, the corresponding token stored locally is no longer usable. To delete the stored token from the local machine, the operator can delete the relevant file under the app's state/ directory.

4. Data the app does not collect

  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No cookies.
  • No personally identifiable information about visitors to this website.

5. Third-party platforms' own policies

When the app communicates with a connected platform's API, that platform's own privacy policy governs any data handled on their side:

  • TikTok: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • YouTube / Google: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Meta / Instagram: https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy

6. Data retention

Locally stored tokens and content remain on the operator's machine until manually deleted. There is no remote storage maintained by the developer.

7. Children's privacy

The app is not intended for use by children under 13. No data is collected about minors.

8. Changes

This policy may be updated. The "Last updated" date above will reflect the most recent revision.
